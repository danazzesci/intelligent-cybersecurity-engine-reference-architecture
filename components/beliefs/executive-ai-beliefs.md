# Well-Intentioned Executive AI Beliefs

These statements reflect **good-faith, fiduciary-motivated beliefs** commonly held by executives overseeing AI systems.  
They are not naïve or reckless assumptions — they are reasonable expectations that **must be testable** to remain defensible.

---

## Executive AI Beliefs

1. **Our AI systems behave within the policies we approved.**

2. **The AI only does what it is authorized to do.**

3. **If the AI were behaving dangerously, we would know.**

4. **The model cannot change its behavior without us approving it.**

5. **The AI respects the same access boundaries as our people and systems.**

6. **We understand what decisions the AI is allowed to influence.**

7. **The AI does not invent facts or guidance without grounding.**

8. **The outputs we rely on are based on approved and trustworthy information.**

9. **Sensitive or proprietary data cannot leak through the AI.**

10. **The AI cannot be manipulated into bypassing our rules.**

11. **If the AI makes a recommendation, it aligns with our stated risk posture.**

12. **Humans remain in control of final decisions.**

13. **The AI will not silently expand its scope or authority.**

14. **If something changes materially, the AI will surface it.**

15. **We can explain how the AI reached an outcome we relied on.**

16. **We could reconstruct what the AI knew and did at the time of an incident.**

17. **Our insurers would view our AI use as responsible and governed.**

18. **Our regulators would see our AI controls as reasonable.**

19. **Our customers would not be harmed by AI behavior we failed to anticipate.**

20. **The AI is accelerating good judgment — not replacing it.**

21. **The AI is not acting outside the intent we originally set.**

22. **If the AI drifts, it will be detected before it matters.**

23. **We are not delegating accountability to the AI.**

24. **We are not relying on the AI to “just catch things.”**

25. **Using AI makes us safer, not just faster.**

---

## Interpretation Note

These beliefs are **reasonable**, **common**, and **well-intentioned**.  
They become dangerous only when they are **assumed rather than tested**.
